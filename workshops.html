Mixtape Sessions

Online Mixtape Sessions: Mixtape Sessions is an online education platform whose purpose is to democratize causal inference by offering courses on causal inference related methodologies (including R, julia and workflow courses) at low prices for students, predocs, postdocs and residents of low and middle income countries ($50/persons) and $595 for everyone else. Scheduled or previous workshops include a 5-day workshop on causal inference (taught by me), a 3-day workshop on difference-in-differences (taught by me), a 1-day workshop instrumental variables (taught by Peter Hull), and a 1-day workshop on difference-in-differences (taught by Jon Roth). Future planned courses (yet to be pinned down) include a course by Grant McDermott (University of Oregon) on workflow, R and version control, Rebecca Thornton (University of Illinois at Urbana Champaign) on field experiments and survey design, Paul HÃ¼ndermund (University of Copenhagen) on causal graphs and causal algorithms, Victor Chernozjukov (MIT) on machine learning and causal inference, and likely more.

In person workshops: But, for those who are interested in having me come visit your department, firm or agency to do a multi-day workshop on causal inference methodology, that too can be worked out. Planned and prior workshops have been at a range of universities and firms such as the Twitch, University of Copenhagen, London School of Economics, Facebook, Manifold, Papa Johns, Oxford University and many more.
