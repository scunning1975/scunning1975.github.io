Workshops

I wrote my book, Causal Inference: the Mixtape, and teach online and in-person workshops to "democratize causal inference" because I believed and still believe that because design-based causal inference spread through applied microeconomists who taught field courses and not usually econometrics that it left many holes in the curriculums across the world in learning this methodology. And because this methodology and worldview has become so dominant, that the training is spotty, even to this day, many economists and young people are still left without access to it. So I started Mixtape Sessions because I deeply want to play a role in the Invisible Hand's filling out of the missing parts. All joking aside, these workshops are designed for everyone, not just economists, including data workers within industry and across all the quantitative social sciences.

Mixtape Sessions
There are two options for my workshops -- the online Mixtape Sessions and the in-person workshops. Mixtape Sessions is my online education platform I created with the purpose to democratize causal inference by offering courses on causal inference related methodologies (including R, julia and workflow courses) at low prices for students, predocs, postdocs and residents of low and middle income countries ($50/persons) and $595 for everyone else. I teach two courses regularly on this platform: a 5-day course on causal inference and a 3-day course on difference-in-differences and if you bundle them, it's $90 for students, predocs, postdocs and residents of low and middle income countries and $995 for everyone else. The content of my workshops are:

Causal Inference 5-day workshop
This workshop is about 90% design-based causal inference and about 10% model based. It covers the foundations of causality (i.e., counterfactuals), potential outcomes, causal graphs (i.e., Judea Pearl) and all the major designs. It includes regular programming exercises in R, python and Stata. The goal is to teach students both the theory of causal inference as well as leave them with code that together instills confidence and competency in building on the course to do their own research in empirical social scientific research in industry, government, public policy and academic applications.

Topics
1. Hidden curriculum on empirical workflow, pre-analysis principles of cleaning and preparation of data, and version control.
2. Foundations in design based causal inference (Card, Angrist and Imbens' worldview focusing the potential outcomes model primarily) and model based causal inference (Judea Pearl's causal graphs)
3. Randomization, selection bias, and randomization inference
4. Regression discontinuity
5. Instrumental variables
6. Difference-in-differences
7. Synthetic control
8. And if possible, matching and weighting


Difference-in-differences and synthetic control 3-day workshop
This 3-day workshop covers a selective review of the more recent advances in DiD, focusing primarily on modeling when there are covariates and when there is differential timing, continuous treatments (i.e., the stuff I like), as well as selective review or recent synthetic control (i.e., the stuff I like).

Topics
1. Potential outcomes review and identification without covariates
2. Identification with covariates
3. Bacon decomposition of twoway fixed effects with differential timing
4. Aggregated group-time approaches to differential timing, including stacking
5. Imputation methods
6. Synthetic controls, matrix completion with nuclear norm regularization, synthetic DiD, and augmented synthetic control
