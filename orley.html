About This Project

The credibility revolution transformed empirical economics by emphasizing causal identification through experimental and quasi-experimental methods. This project traces the intellectual lineage from Orley Ashenfelter at Princeton through his students and their descendants, documenting how these methods spread through the profession.

The data comes from multiple sources: hand-collected CV data, the Mathematics Genealogy Project, and crowdsourced contributions. If you're part of this academic family tree, please contribute your information below.

Methodology

Data Sources: Original data was hand-collected from CVs and faculty websites. Generation 3 data was scraped from the Mathematics Genealogy Project. Additional data comes from crowdsourced submissions.

Inclusion Criteria: We track primary advisors, co-advisors, and committee members. The "student count" for Gen 1 advisors includes all committee relationships (e.g., Katz's 257 includes students where he served on committees).

Known Limitations: Math Genealogy only captures primary advisors, so co-advising relationships are undercounted. Some recent graduates (2024-2025) may be missing. Non-academic placements are harder to track.
