About Me

I am a professor of economics at Baylor University. I study a number of topics including mental healthcare, sex work, abortion and drug policy. I love my family, friends, colleagues, students, HBO, Always Sunny in Philadelphia, causal inference, Mac Miller and Kanye West.

Check out my free online textbook, Causal Inference: the Mixtape (with accompanying Spotify playlist). Yale University Press is publishing a beautiful physical copy, which is available for preorder now, and physical delivery on January 26, 2021. I have updated and expanded each chapter to make the book more contemporary. In addition, I now include programming examples in R, as well as Stata. I think it turned out really well, so you should totally buy it. You can purchase the book from such vendors as Amazon and Yale University Press for a mere $35! That's like buying an unusually expensive cup of coffee!

In addition to a hard copy book, Yale has graciously agree to continue publishing a free version. On January 3, 2021, I will will also post a free online HTML version of the Mixtape to my website. The free HTML version will be identical to the physical book but also include interactive R programming examples that can be run from the browser, which I think is pretty slick. It will be easy on the eyes and beautiful to gaze upon. If you like it, consider buying it, unless your willingness to pay for the hard copy is less than $35, in which case don't. Either way, the online HTML version is free and for the people.
